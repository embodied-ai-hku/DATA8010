Course Description

This course explores various topics in Embodied Artificial Intelligence (AI), focusing on the perception and representation of the physical world by autonomous agents and their consequent physical interactions.

Specifically, we will cover:

  • How an agent can infer physical and semantic states of a scene (e.g., via 3D reconstruction and semantic parsing).
  • How these perceptions can be represented to facilitate efficient and explainable reasoning.
  • How actions can be generated to achieve downstream interaction tasks.

Prerequisites: Basic knowledge of linear algebra, probability, random processes, optimization, signal processing, and machine learning.
